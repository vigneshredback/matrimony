History of the English Language

All living things on our planet have progenitors from whom they receive certain characteristics. Not just live animals, but even non-living entities such as languages, are subject to inheritance. Each language comes from a separate set of families, and they borrow many terms for daily items from one another.

There are about 250-300 idiosyncratic language families in the world. Some of them are listed below for your knowledge.

  • i. Austronesian
  • ii. Indo-European
  • iii. Sino-Tibetan
  • iv. Niger-Congo
  • v. Dravidian

In the present decade, two to three billion people speak Modern English across the globe. Hold on! Does anyone know where the English language comes from? Many questions would pop up if you think deeply.
